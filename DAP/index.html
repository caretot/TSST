Understanding Data Priorities

Priority 1 Data (Green): Trip to change to back-up tool. Can only be dropped with the agreement of the Wells Superintendent, Rig-Line SPA, Depletion Management TL, New Well Delivery TL and Drilling Engineering TL. Completions Engineering TL to be consulted.

Priority 2 Data (Yellow): Proceed without this data if the tool fails but change out if the opportunity arises. Nevertheless, Wells Superintendent, Rig-Line SPA, Depletion Management TL, New Well Delivery TL and Drilling Engineering TL may elect to acquire the data, depending on the criticality of the log. Completions Engineering TL to be consulted.

Note (*): Conditional activation. Subject to be activated during drilling if needed. For ALD Image specifically: can be activated during drilling if the need is identified.

How This Tool Works

Overview

This Well Data Acquisition Plan tool helps you systematically determine which LWD (Logging While Drilling) and reservoir characterization tools are required for your well sections. The tool uses a question-based approach to intelligently assign Priority 1, Priority 2, or "Not Required" status to each logging tool.

Step 1: Define Your Well Design

Start by adding hole sections with their respective sizes in the "Well Design - Hole Sections" area. For example:

  • 22" x 30" HS - Surface hole with back-reamer
  • 9 5/8" - Single size intermediate section
  • 14 ¾" x 17" - Open hole with liner

You can drag sections to reorder them, and delete sections with the ✕ button.

Step 2: Answer Tool-Specific Questions

For each hole section, you'll see tabs in the "Tool Requirements by Hole Section" area. Each tool has specific questions (Yes/No/Maybe responses). The questions are designed to assess whether the tool is:

  • Critical (Priority 1): Essential for well objectives
  • Important (Priority 2): Valuable but not critical
  • Not Required: Not needed for this section

Step 3: Automatic Priority Assignment

The system automatically calculates priority based on your answers:

  • ≥60% "Yes" responses: Priority 1 (Green)
  • ≥30% "Yes" responses: Priority 2 (Yellow)
  • <30% "Yes" responses: Not Required (White)

These thresholds can be adjusted based on your specific project requirements.

Step 4: Review & Customize

The Data Acquisition Plan table shows all your decisions at a glance. You can:

  • Click any cell to manually edit or override the automatic assignment
  • Mark tools as conditional (*) if they can be activated during drilling
  • Generate a professional narrative summary of your logging plan

Step 5: Export Your Plan

Once satisfied with your plan, export it in your preferred format:

  • Excel: Data table for spreadsheet analysis
  • Word: Formatted document with table and summary
  • PDF: Print-ready document

Tips & Best Practices

  • Save your work frequently by exporting to PDF or Word
  • Use the "Maybe" response when tool criticality depends on real-time decisions
  • Review Priority 1 selections with your team to ensure alignment
  • Update the summary to reflect any manual adjustments made to the table
  • Share exported documents with stakeholders for approval
