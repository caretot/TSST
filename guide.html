Overview

The Well Planning Suite is a browser-based toolkit for computing geometric relationships between a drilled well and a subsurface formation. It is designed for exploration and development geologists and drilling engineers who need quick, transparent calculations without a spreadsheet.

All calculations assume a straight-line (no-curvature) well trajectory and a planar formation. No curvature correction is applied.

Key Concepts

Input Angles & Directions

FD — Formation DipAngle of the formation measured down from horizontal. Range 0–90°. A horizontal bed has FD = 0°; a vertical formation has FD = 90°.
FA — Formation AzimuthDirection of dip, measured clockwise from North. Range 0–360°. This is the dip direction, not the strike. Strike = FA − 90°.
WI — Well InclinationAngle of the well measured up from vertical. Range 0–90°. A vertical well has WI = 0°; a horizontal well has WI = 90°.
WA — Well AzimuthDirection the well is heading, measured clockwise from North. Range 0–360°.
ℹ Formation dip is measured down from horizontal and well inclination is measured up from vertical. This is the standard convention used in the suite and matches the original Excel tool by Giuseppe D'Antonio.

Thickness Types

TSTTrue Stratigraphic Thickness — perpendicular distance between formation top and base, measured normal to the bedding plane.
MDMeasured Depth — length of wellbore drilled through the formation.
VTVertical Thickness in Well — vertical component of MD. VT = MD × cos(WI).
TVTTrue Vertical Thickness — vertical distance between formation top and base measured straight down. TVT = TST / cos(FD).
TVDTrue Vertical Depth — absolute vertical depth from surface. Used for reservoir top, GOC, and OWC inputs.

Core Formulas

Intersection Angle α

The apparent dip — the angle between the well axis and the bedding plane. This is the fundamental geometric quantity that all other calculations derive from.

α = arcsin( |w · n| )

where w = well unit vector  =  [ sin(WI)·cos(WA),  sin(WI)·sin(WA),  −cos(WI) ]

and  n = formation normal =  [ sin(FD)·cos(FA),  sin(FD)·sin(FA),  cos(FD) ]
⚠ When α ≈ 0° the well runs parallel to bedding — no valid MD/TST conversion exists. The suite flags this as a parallel geometry.

MD / TST Conversion

MD = TST / sin(α)
TST = MD × sin(α)

Derived Thicknesses

VT  = MD × cos(WI)
TVT = TST / cos(FD)
TST / MD  = sin(α)
TST / TVT = cos(FD)

Reservoir Geometry

Base Reservoir TVD = Top Reservoir TVD + VT
Formation Strike = (FA − 90 + 360) mod 360

Tools

Well Intersection Calculator
1
Enter FD, FA, WI, WA in the Well & Formation Parameters panel. Values are clamped to their valid ranges automatically.
2
Enter Top Reservoir TVD, GOC, and OWC depths as positive numbers. The tool converts them to negative internally for plotting.
3
In the MD Calculator panel enter a TST value. All derived quantities update instantly: MD, VT, TVT, ratios, base reservoir depth, and strike.
4
In the TST Calculator panel enter an MD to convert in the other direction.
5
The Trajectory Plot shows the wellbore (red), formation top (grey solid), base (grey dashed), GOC (pink dashed), and OWC (cyan dashed). Use the Refresh button if the canvas looks distorted after resizing.
6
The Rose Diagram shows compass orientation. Red = formation azimuth (length ∝ 1 − FD/90). Violet dashed = dip direction (FA + 90°, same length scaling). Grey = wellbore (length ∝ 1 − WI/90).
Dip & Azimuth Sweep
1
Set Well Inclination and Well Azimuth, then enter the given TST (in TST→MD mode) or MD (in MD→TST mode).
2
Define the Formation Dip sweep range (Lowest / Highest in whole degrees). The X-axis covers every 1° step in this range.
3
Define the Formation Azimuth sweep range. Up to 14 evenly-spaced azimuth values are plotted as separate colour-coded series.
4
The Intersection Angle bar shows the angle at the midpoint of both sweep ranges.
5
In the 3-Case Matrix below, enter Min / Base / Max values for both FD and FA independently. The 3 × 3 = 9 result cards show intersection angle and MD/TST for every combination.
✓ Red cells in the matrix mean the well runs nearly parallel to bedding at that geometry. The actual near-zero angle is shown with a ⚠ warning rather than just "N/A".
TST ↔ MD Monte Carlo Simulator
1
For each of the five parameters (TST/MD, FD, FA, WI, WA) enter Minimum, Most Likely, and Maximum values defining a triangular distribution.
2
Choose the number of iterations (1 000 – 50 000). More iterations give a smoother CDF at the cost of runtime.
3
Click CALCULATE. The simulator draws random samples from each triangular distribution, computes MD (or TST) for every sample, and builds the CDF.
4
The Percentile Table and Stat Cards give P10–P90, mean, and standard deviation. Hover the CDF chart to read off MD/TST, CDF%, and apparent dip α at any point.
5
The Sensitivity Matrix performs a One-At-a-Time (OAT) sweep: each parameter varies across its Min/ML/Max while all others stay at Most Likely, showing which parameter has the largest impact on the intersection angle.
6
Use the Copy / Paste buttons in the Input panel header to save and restore all 15 input values across sessions or between modes.

Tips & Notes

ℹ Azimuth convention: All azimuths are geographic (true North), measured clockwise 0–360°. Formation azimuth is the dip direction, not the strike. Strike = FA − 90°.
⚠ Depth sign convention: Reservoir depths (Top, GOC, OWC) are entered as positive numbers. The app takes the absolute value internally, so entering negative values is also fine.
✓ Live updates: The Well Intersection Calculator and Dip & Az Sweep update automatically on every input change. The Monte Carlo tool requires clicking CALCULATE because each run involves thousands of random samples.
✓ Trajectory plot distortion: If the cross-section plot looks stretched after resizing your browser window, click the Refresh button in the panel header to force a clean redraw at the correct pixel ratio.
