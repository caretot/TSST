Browser-based well geometry toolkit

Features
Exact geometric intersection angle
Uses the full 3D dot-product formula between the well unit vector and the formation normal. No approximations. Works for any combination of FD, FA, WI, WA.
Monte Carlo uncertainty propagation
Triangular-distribution sampling across all five input parameters with up to 50 000 iterations. Outputs a full CDF with P5–P95 percentiles, mean, and standard deviation.
Formation dip & azimuth sweep
Plots MD (or TST) as a family of curves across a full range of dip and azimuth values. Up to 14 azimuth series. Includes a 3×3 case matrix for Min / Base / Max combinations.
Trajectory cross-section & rose diagram
DPR-aware canvas rendering of the wellbore in cross-section with formation top, base, GOC, and OWC. Compass rose shows formation azimuth, dip direction, and wellbore orientation with geometrically proportional line lengths.
Runs entirely in the browser
No server, no login, no data upload. All computation happens locally in JavaScript. Your well data never leaves your machine.

Assumptions & Limitations

The suite implements the geometric model originally described by Simon Stewart (October 2006) and extended by Giuseppe D'Antonio (September 2009 / March 2010).

· All angles are in degrees.
· Assumes no curvature of the wellbore or formation — straight well, planar bed.
· Formation dip is measured down from horizontal.
· Well inclination is measured up from vertical.
· Formation azimuth is the dip direction (not strike). Strike = FA − 90°.
· Depths are entered as positive TVD values; signs are handled internally.
· The trajectory cross-section uses apparent dip projected into the wellbore azimuth plane — this is the geologically correct view for a vertical section.
